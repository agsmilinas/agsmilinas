Short bio

I'm a Ph.D. candidate in Computer Science at McGill University, affiliated with MILA (Quebec AI Institute) and The Neuro, where my research focuses on scalable, biologically informed modeling frameworks built on high-resolution multimodal data. My path began with a B.Sc. in Mechatronics Engineering from ITESM and a Master's in Computer Science and Engineering from UNAM, where I built a real-time sign-language translator using deep convolutional networks.

I've been a teaching assistant at McGill for two years across COMP 310 (Operating Systems), COMP 208 (Computer Programming for the Physical Sciences and Engineering), and COMP 307 (Principles of Web Development), as well as at the Université de Montréal, and served as a guest professor in Big Data & Analytics at Universidad Iberoamericana.

Alongside academia I bring over a decade of industry experience — Lead Data Scientist at Distillery, AI/ML Cloud Solutions Engineer at Google, and Senior Data Scientist at Coca-Cola, with earlier roles at IBM, Lumiata, and P&G — deploying scalable AI systems and leading cross-functional teams across machine learning, big data, and cloud infrastructure.

My expertise spans deep learning, computer vision, NLP, and responsible AI. I'm driven by bridging rigorous research with real-world impact — advanced simulation models, AI for medical imaging, and multimodal integration — and turning those into technologies that serve science and society.

Paper
RAG-Safe — Accepted at Canadian AI 2026

Our paper RAG-Safe (Paper ID 338) was accepted at the 39th Canadian Conference on Artificial Intelligence, held May 25–29, 2026 at Simon Fraser University, Vancouver.

RAG-Safe is a recall-first, retrieval-augmented content-moderation framework, comparing two pipeline designs on the Jigsaw toxicity corpus while characterizing the precision trade-offs of each architecture.

Paper
Bifurcation Preservation — AI4Physics @ ICML 2026

Accepted at the AI4Physics Workshop at ICML 2026 (Seoul, July 6–11, 2026): Bifurcation Preservation as a Physics Diagnostic for Neural Phase-Field Surrogates.

The work shows a neural-operator surrogate can reach ~9× lower rollout error than a baseline while still shifting the dissolve/persist bifurcation boundary — evidence that low reconstruction error can mask altered dynamics.

Paper
NeuroAgents — Accepted at IEEE EMBC 2026

Our full paper (ID 1920) was accepted at the 48th IEEE EMBC, July 26–30, 2026, Toronto: NeuroAgents: LLM-Based Orchestration of High-Resolution Brain Dynamics Simulations from Natural Language.

Alejandro Salinas-Medina, Anisleidy Gonzalez-Mitjans, Paule-Joanne Toussaint, Alan Evans, Xue Liu. It scales to large vertex counts where conventional simulators exhaust memory, while maintaining high tool-selection accuracy.
