Short Bio

Alejandro Salinas-Medina is an accomplished Ph.D. candidate at McGill University, with nearly a decade of industry experience in AI, machine learning, big data, and cloud computing. He has worked with prominent global organizations such as Google, Coca-Cola, and IBM, contributing as a Big Data AI/ML Cloud Technical Solutions Engineer, Senior Data Scientist, and Full Stack Developer/Machine Learning Engineer.

His robust industry expertise is complemented by his academic background, which includes a Master's in Computer Science and Engineering from UNAM (best ranked research university in Mexico), and his current research is centered on the development of scalable modeling frameworks through the integration of high-resolution multimodal data.

His unique ability to bridge academic research with industry solutions positions him at the cutting edge of computational modelling and AI innovation.

    • Poster and Flash Talk at MAIN 2024 (https://www.main2024.org):

      "Neuron-Level Analysis of the BigBrain Dataset"

      Alejandro Salinas-Medina, Andrija Å tajduhar, Claude Y. Lepage, Paule Toussaint, Xue Liu, Alan Evans

      McGill University

      Contact: Google Scholar

      This study presents a comprehensive framework for conducting neuron-level analysis of the BigBrain dataset, focusing on 1-micron sections. The methodology involves selecting and tiling specific dataset sections, followed by segmentation and morphological feature extraction from each tile. These data are standardized by transforming them into a registered space, allowing for consistent analysis across sections. Point cloud features such as nearest neighbor, 3D convex hull, and second-order features are used to analyze the spatial organization and structural properties of neurons. The results provide deeper insights into neuronal architecture at the microscale, contributing to both neuroscience and computational modeling fields.
